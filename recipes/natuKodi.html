NATU KODI

Description

Natu Kodi Kura is a popular country chicken curry recipe that is part of Andhra cuisine. Natu Kodi, meaning country chicken is a rich gravy made with different spices. The typical village-style recipe is cooked on a wood fire in mud pots. All the ingredients are mixed and slow-cooked to perfection for a delicious, smoky flavour. Natu Kodi is enjoyed with minapa garelu, also known as medu vada. However, you can also have it with rice or roti as well. For that authentic taste, it’s important to use country chicken and not broiler chicken. For this recipe, you can choose to cut the chicken either into small pieces or larger-sized pieces.

Ingredients

  • 2 tbsp poppy seeds
  • 1 tbsp Coriander Seeds
  • 2 tsp Guntur chilli powder
  • tbsp ½grated coconut
  • 2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 6 to 7 ginger slices
  • 6 to 7 garlic cloves
  • 1 tsp salt
  • A little bit of water
  • For the chicken
  • 7 pieces to 8of country chicken
  • 1 tsp chilli powder
  • tsp ½turmeric powder
  • 3 tbsp of oil
  • For the base
  • Few curry leaves
  • 4 green chillies
  • 1 finely chopped onion
  • 1 cups ½of water
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 3 tbsp to 4oil
  • Salt - to taste
  • Coriander leaves to garnish

Steps

  1. In a pan, add the poppy seeds, coriander seeds, Guntur chilli powder, coconut, garlic, ginger, turmeric powder and salt. Dry roast all the ingredients well.
  2. In a mortar and pestle, add the roasted ingredients and grind with a little water to form a paste. Keep aside.
  3. In a pressure cooker, heat some oil. Add the chicken pieces and sear. Add turmeric and chilli powder.
  4. Add a little water and cook for about four to five whistles.
  5. In a pot or wok, add oil, curry leaves, green chillies, and onions. Sauté until the onions are soft and brown.
  6. Add the cooked chicken and the spice paste. Sauté for about three to four minutes.
  7. Add water, salt and garam masala. Allow the gravy to simmer for a few minutes.
  8. Turn off the heat and garnish with coriander leaves. Serve hot. Enjoy!
